# Kerf Go-To-Market Strategy

## Global Customer Acquisition Playbook

---

## Executive Summary

Kerf sits at the intersection of massive pain ($86K-235K/year compliance cost), an empty market ($99-200/month construction-specific space nobody occupies), and 750K+ potential customers in the US alone. International markets (UK, AU, CA) add another 800K+ small contractors.

This strategy is designed for **near-zero spend with ROI within 3 months**, leveraging AI agent automation, product-led growth, and a viral GC-to-sub network loop.

**The #1 insight:** The product IS the marketing channel. Every document Kerf generates travels through the construction ecosystem — printed on jobsites, submitted to GCs, reviewed by inspectors, shared with insurers. The GC Portal viral loop is the single highest-leverage growth mechanism.

## Part 1: The GC Viral Loop (Priority #1)

This is the growth engine that makes everything else work. One GC acquisition cascades into dozens of sub acquisitions.

### How It Works

```
GC signs up (free portal)
  -> Mandates subs submit docs via Kerf
    -> 80+ subs receive invitation emails
      -> Subs discover the platform
        -> 8-12% convert to paid within 12 months
          -> Those subs work for OTHER GCs
            -> New GCs discover Kerf
              -> Cycle repeats (viral coefficient ~1.0-1.2)
```

### GC Acquisition (The First 10)

**Target:** Mid-market commercial GCs (50-500 employees) in ONE metro. Pick Houston, Dallas, Atlanta, or Phoenix (high volume, strong OSHA enforcement).

**Entry point:** The Safety Director — the person tracking sub compliance in spreadsheets.

**The pitch:**
> "You're already responsible when your subs get OSHA citations. You're already liable when a sub's worker gets hurt on your jobsite. You're already spending 10 hours a week chasing insurance certs and safety plans by email. Kerf gives you a single dashboard showing which subs are compliant and which aren't — updated in real time. Your subs upload their own documents. You stop chasing. It costs you nothing."

**Critical decision: GC Portal is FREE.** The GC is the distribution channel, not the customer. Revenue comes from subs who upgrade. Zero friction at the seed node = maximum viral spread.

### Sub Onboarding Flow

The invitation email is the single most important piece of copy:
- **Subject:** "Action Required: [GC Name] — Safety Compliance Documentation"
- Uses GC's logo prominently (sub opens it because they recognize their client)
- Lists exact documents needed and a time estimate ("5-10 minutes")
- CTA is "Upload Your Documents" not "Sign Up for Kerf"

**Conversion from forced compliance to willing adoption:**
1. **Weeks 1-4 (passive value):** Compliance summary emails, expiration alerts
2. **Months 2-6 (multi-GC value):** Second GC invites same sub — docs auto-populate. "I didn't have to email it again." This reframes Kerf from "something ABC made me use" to "my compliance hub"
3. **Months 3-12 (proactive value):** Sub discovers document generation, mock inspections, worker cert tracking. Upgrade moment arrives.

### Free Sub Tier vs Paid

| Free (forever) | Paid (upgrade triggers) |
|---|---|
| Respond to GC compliance requests | Generate safety documents (SSSP, JHA, toolbox talks) |
| Upload/store core documents | Incident reporting and tracking |
| Expiration reminders | Worker training management |
| Basic compliance profile | Multi-project management |
| View compliance score | Analytics, mock OSHA inspections |

### Document Branding (Zero-Cost Distribution)

Every generated document includes a subtle professional footer:
```
Generated with Kerf | kerf.build
Document ID: SF-2026-04-A7B3C2
```

Prequalification submissions include a verification URL. When a GC clicks it, they see the sub's verified compliance status — and an invitation to manage ALL their subs through Kerf.

QR codes on printed jobsite documents link to digital versions with a soft CTA below.

### Network Acceleration Tactics

- **Multi-GC sharing:** Subs can share their compliance profile with ALL their GCs — the viral transmission event
- **Compliance scoring (0-100):** GCs see subs ranked. Creates competitive pressure for subs to maintain high scores
- **Kerf Verified badge:** Score above 85 for 90 days. Downloadable for websites, bids, email signatures
- **Geographic density:** Saturate one metro at a time (10 GCs -> 500+ subs -> network effects kick in -> expand to next metro)

### Viral Loop Metrics

| Metric | Month 6 Target | Month 18 Target |
|---|---|---|
| Subs invited per GC | 50 | 80 |
| Sub signup rate | 25% | 35% |
| Sub activation (uploads docs) | 18% | 28% |

**Unit economics:** At month 18: 80 subs invited per GC, 6 upgrade to paid at $99/mo avg = $594/mo revenue per GC acquired. At $2K acquisition cost = 3.4 month payback. Plus one organic GC referral.

## Part 2: Free Acquisition Channels

### A. Product-Led Growth: Free Tools (Launch First)

These capture high-intent leads at zero cost.

**1. OSHA Penalty Calculator**
- Input violation type, get estimated fine amount
- Email-gated for detailed report
- Target keywords: "OSHA fine calculator", "how much does OSHA fine for [violation]" (2K-8K monthly searches)
- Build time: 2 days. Cost: $0
- Expected: 50-200 leads/month once indexed

**2. Safety Program Gap Checker**
- 10-question assessment, outputs compliance score + gap analysis
- Claude API generates personalized PDF report (~$0.03/report)
- Build time: 3 days. Cost: ~$5-15/month
- Expected: 100-300 leads/month

**3. Free Toolbox Talk Generator**
- One free generation per email per week
- Leverages existing generation engine
- Build time: 1 day. Cost: ~$2-5/month
- Expected: 200-500 leads/month (toolbox talks = #1 searched safety topic)

**4. Free Mock OSHA Inspection (Core PLG)**
- 10-minute self-assessment. Score + gap list
- The gap list IS the sales pitch — shows exactly what Kerf fixes
- Free: top-level score. Email-gated: full report. Paid: AI remediation plan
- Expected: 15-25% completion -> email capture -> 5-10% -> trial -> 15-25% -> paid

### B. Content & SEO

**YouTube (Primary Content Channel)**
Contractors watch YouTube constantly. They don't read blogs.

- **"60-Second Safety Briefing"** — 3-5x/week, one hazard per video. Cost: $0-200/month
- **"Mock OSHA Walk"** — walk through jobsites pointing out violations. Gets shared in group chats and Facebook groups organically
- **"OSHA Citation Breakdown"** — real public citations. "This Roofer Just Got Fined $156K." Pure SEO gold
- **"Seguridad en 2 Minutos"** — parallel Spanish content. Almost zero competition

**TikTok/Reels/Shorts**
- "OSHA Fine or Fine?" — show jobsite photo, viewers guess if it's a violation. Engagement bait + educational
- "Rate My Jobsite" — submitted photos, roast the violations
- "How much would OSHA fine this?" — guess the penalty
- Cost: $0. Time to traction: 4-8 weeks

**SEO: Programmatic Landing Pages**
- 15 trades x 50 states = 750 pages targeting "OSHA compliance for [trade] in [state]"
- Low competition, high intent (50-500 monthly searches each)
- Each page includes penalty calculator + gap checker CTA
- Generation cost: ~$112 total (Claude API). Expected: 5K-15K organic visits/month at steady state

**Spanish-Language SEO**
- Near zero competition in Spanish construction safety content
- "requisitos de seguridad OSHA en construccion", "charlas de seguridad temas construccion"
- Enormous underserved audience (34% of construction workforce is Hispanic/Latino)

### C. Community & Social

**Facebook Groups (Where Contractors Actually Are)**
- Key groups: "Construction Contractors" (100K+), state-specific groups, trade-specific groups, Spanish-language groups
- Strategy: Weeks 1-4 answer questions helpfully. Weeks 5-8 share YouTube content. Weeks 9-12 share free tools. Never hard-sell
- Cost: $0. Time: 30-60 min/day. Expected: 20-50 free tool signups/week by month 3

**Reddit:** r/Construction (300K+), r/OSHA, trade-specific subs. Pure value-first approach

**LinkedIn:** Secondary channel targeting GC safety directors and insurance brokers

### D. Partnerships (Months 3-6)

**Insurance Carriers/Agents (Highest Value)**
- Safety compliance reduces claims. Insurers have financial incentive to push Kerf
- Start with independent agents specializing in construction (thousands of them)
- Agent recommends Kerf during renewals: "Improve your EMR, your premium drops"
- Structure: referral commission or co-branded landing page

**Trade Associations**
- ABC (21K members), AGC (27K members), NAHB (140K members), plus local chapters
- Free safety workshops at chapter meetings. Member-exclusive discounts. Newsletter content
- Cost: $0-2K per association

**Safety Training Providers**
- OSHA 10/30 trainers recommend Kerf as the implementation tool post-training
- "You just completed OSHA 30 — now use Kerf to maintain compliance year-round"
- Revenue share: 10-20% recurring commission

## Part 3: AI Agent Automation System

### The Machine ($250-300/month total operating cost)

- Monitors OSHA enforcement data daily via free public API (`enforcedata.dol.gov`)

- Yields: 200-400 citations/week
- Enriches via Google Places API (free tier), Apollo.io
- Generates personalized outreach referencing specific violations
- **Cost:** $55/month. **Output:** 150-300 qualified leads/month

#### Agent 2: Personalized Cold Email Engine
- Takes enriched leads, Claude generates hyper-personalized 4-email sequences
- Sent via Instantly.ai ($30/month) with 3 warm-up domains
- Each email references the contractor's specific violation, trade, location
- **Expected:** 3K-5K emails/month, 2-5% reply rate = 60-250 conversations/month

**Email 1 (Day 0) — The Question:**
> "When a GC asks for your site-specific safety plan, how long does it take you to put it together? ... P.S. Give me a project name and I'll generate a free SSSP and send it over. No strings."

**Email 3 (Day 9) — The Story:**
> "A [trade] contractor in [nearby city] signed up after losing a $400K bid because their safety docs weren't ready by the GC's deadline..."

#### Agent 3: Content Generation Engine
- Generates daily social content from real OSHA data + construction news
- Monday: "This Week's OSHA Focus." Wednesday: "Citation Spotlight." Friday: "Weekend Safety Read"
- Bilingual (English + Spanish) for all social posts
- Queued in Buffer ($6/month)
- **Cost:** $45/month. **Output:** 30 social posts, 4 newsletters, 20 video scripts/month

#### Agent 4: SEO Page Generator
- Generates 750 trade x state landing pages
- Each targeting long-tail keywords with embedded lead capture tools
- Monthly refresh of stats and regulatory updates
- **One-time cost:** ~$112. **Ongoing:** $20/month for refreshes

#### Agent 5: Trial Activation & Churn Prevention
- Monitors trial user behavior, triggers interventions:
  - Day 1 no project created -> "Let me set up your first project"

- **Cost:** $0-49/month (PostHog free tier + Resend)

#### Agent 6: Building Permit Monitor (Month 3+)

- Identifies contractors pulling new commercial permits
- Outreach: "Congrats on the new project at [address]. Need a site-specific safety plan?"
- **Cost:** $20/month. **Output:** 100-300 leads/month

## Part 4: International Expansion

### Market Sequencing (Do NOT Launch Simultaneously)

| Phase | Market | Timeline | Why First |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Canada (Alberta-first) | Months 1-3 | Lowest competition, strongest ROI story (COR = WCB rebates), most similar to US |
| 2 | UK | Months 3-6 | Large underserved SME market, mandatory RAMS documentation, CHAS/SSIP accreditation trigger |
| 3 | Australia | Months 6-9 | Good fundamentals but SafetyCulture's home turf — requires strongest differentiation |

### Canada: COR is the Killer Feature

**COR (Certificate of Recognition)** is practically mandatory for Alberta contractors bidding on oil & gas or public infrastructure. COR-certified companies receive **5-15% WCB premium refunds** — often worth tens of thousands per year.

**Messaging:** "COR-ready in weeks, not months. Save thousands on WCB premiums. Bid on bigger projects."

**Channels:**
- COR Audit Preparation Guide (lead magnet) — free checklist attracts high-intent traffic
- Partner with ACSA (Alberta Construction Safety Association)
- Google Ads on "COR certification help," "COR audit preparation" — low competition
- ISNetworld compliance guides (heavily used in Canadian oil & gas)
- **Cost:** ~$1,500 CAD/month. **Target:** 10-20 paying customers by month 1

### UK: CHAS/SSIP is the Door Opener

UK subs need CHAS/SSIP accreditation to win work. This costs 500-700 GBP/year and requires documented safety management systems, risk assessments, method statements.

**Messaging:** "Get CHAS-ready. Win more work. Stop drowning in RAMS paperwork."

**Competitor gap:** HandsHQ does RAMS generation but lacks mock inspections, voice-first tools, and multi-jurisdiction coverage. HASpod does templates but not AI generation.

**Channels:**
- CHAS/SSIP document templates (lead magnet)
- Facebook groups ("Builders Talk Group," "UK Builders and Contractors")
- Partner with FMB (Federation of Master Builders, ~8K members)
- Trade press PR (Construction Enquirer, Building magazine)
- Safety consultant reseller program
- **Cost:** ~$1,500 GBP/month. **Target:** 20-30 customers by month 2

### Australia: SWMS is the Entry Point

Safe Work Method Statements (SWMS) are legally required for 19 categories of high-risk construction work. Every small contractor searches for SWMS templates.

**Messaging:** "Generate SWMS in minutes, not hours. Keep SafeWork off your back."

**Competitor gap:** SafetyCulture dominates inspections but does NOT generate SWMS. No dominant AI-first safety doc generator for SME contractors.

**Channels:**
- SWMS template library (lead magnet for all 19 high-risk categories)
- Facebook groups (Australian tradie groups are extremely active)
- Partner with MBA state chapters (Master Builders, 32K+ members)
- Tradie influencer partnerships (500-2K AUD per mention)
- **Cost:** ~$2K AUD/month. **Target:** 5-10 customers by month 3

### Multi-Jurisdiction Moat

Each jurisdiction Kerf adds makes it harder for competitors to catch up. A contractor operating in Alberta AND Ontario AND BC needs one platform for all three. The regulatory knowledge across US + UK + AU + CA with provincial/state variation would take a competitor 12-18 months minimum to replicate.

## Part 5: Messaging & Sales Playbook

### Core Value Propositions (Ranked)

**Lead with Variant B for ads (emotional), A for sales (financial), C for email (urgency):**

- **A (Money):** "Stop paying consultants $86K a year to do what an app can do in 10 minutes."
- **B (Time):** "Get your Sunday nights back. Kerf writes your safety docs while you're still on the jobsite."
- **C (Fear):** "The next OSHA inspector doesn't care that you're a good contractor. He cares what's on paper."
- **D (Opportunity):** "You're losing bids right now because your safety program looks like it was stapled together at Kinko's."
- **E (Identity):** "You run a tight crew. Your safety program should look like it."

### The Bar Conversation

> "You know how every GC wants a full safety program before they'll even let you bid? And how you end up spending your Sunday night copying and pasting templates, or paying a consultant $2K a month?
>
> We built an app that does it for you. Answer a few questions, it spits out the whole thing — SSSP, JHA, toolbox talks, the works. Like having a safety director on your phone for less than your diesel fill-up.
>
> The guys in the field do inspections by talking into their phone. English or Spanish. And it tracks everybody's certs so you don't find out someone's OSHA 10 expired when the inspector shows up."

### Messages That DON'T Work

- "Compliance management solution" — say "safety stuff" or "paperwork"
- "Leverage AI to optimize your safety outcomes" — sounds like someone who's never been on a jobsite
- "Streamline your workflows" — say "stop doing the same paperwork twice"
- "Digital transformation" — instant tune-out
- "Empower your workforce" — foremen want to go home on time
- Don't lead with AI. Lead with what it does. AI is the how, not the why
- Don't imply they're doing safety wrong. Lead with "make it easier" not "fix your mess"

### Key Objection Handling

**"We've been fine without it."**
> "Most guys who call us were fine too — right up until they weren't. Usually one of three things: surprise OSHA visit, lost bid, or workers comp through the roof. We're a lot cheaper before than after. Quick question — when was the last time a GC asked for your safety program and you had to scramble?"

**"My guys won't use it."**
> "That's why the field stuff is all voice. Foreman pulls out his phone, hits record, says what he sees. Done. Works in Spanish too. Can your guys send a voice text? It's that easy."

**"We already have a safety consultant."**
> "How much? ... I'm not saying fire them. Keep them for audits and training. Use Kerf for all the daily docs, inspections, and reports they charge hourly for. Cut their hours in half. That savings alone pays for Kerf ten times over."

**"It's too expensive."**
> "One serious OSHA citation is $16,131. One willful is $161,323. Average workers comp claim is $41K. Safety consultants run $2-4K/month. Kerf starts at $99/month. That's less than your porta-john rental."

**"I'll just use templates from the internet."**
> "OSHA doesn't want a generic template with your logo. They want a program specific to your company, projects, hazards, and crew. Also — if they see the same template as the guy down the street, that's a red flag, not a check mark."

### The Aha Moment (Demo)

**Generate a Site-Specific Safety Plan live in 90 seconds.** Ask the prospect for a current project. Type it in. Watch their face when a 30-page professional document appears. This is the moment that sells the product.

**Leave-behind:** Send them the generated SSSP for their actual project. "It's yours to use whether or not you sign up."

### Trigger Events (When to Reach Out)

| Tier | Event | Response Time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (calling today) | OSHA citation, injury on site, lost bid due to safety docs | Immediate |
| 2 (open to conversation) | Workers comp renewal in 30-60 days, stricter GC requirements, hired 10+ employees | Within a week |
| 3 (seed planting) | OSHA emphasis program announced, competitor cited, trade show season, January planning | Ongoing content |

**Best months to sell:** Jan, Feb, Mar, Sep, Oct
**Best months to plant seeds:** Jul (heat emphasis fear), Nov (year-end reflection)
**Hardest months:** May, Jun (too busy building)
