Executive Summary

Kerf sits at the intersection of massive pain ($86K–235K/year compliance cost), an empty market ($99–200/month construction-specific space nobody occupies), and 750K+ potential customers in the US alone. International markets add another 800K+ small contractors.

The #1 Insight

The product IS the marketing channel. Every document Kerf generates travels through the construction ecosystem. The GC Portal viral loop is the single highest-leverage growth mechanism.

Fully Autonomous — Zero Human Contact

This strategy requires zero phone calls, zero meetings, zero conferences, zero in-person activity. Every channel runs through Hermes and AI agents. Hermes logs into platforms (Facebook, LinkedIn, email), engages autonomously, and learns what converts. The only human input is reviewing Hermes' weekly performance report and approving strategic direction changes.

1. The GC Viral Loop (Priority #1)

One GC acquisition cascades into dozens of sub acquisitions.

GC signs up (free portal) → Mandates subs submit docs via Kerf → 80+ subs receive invitation emails → Subs discover the platform → 8–12% convert to paid within 12 months → Those subs work for OTHER GCs → New GCs discover Kerf → Cycle repeats (k ~1.0–1.2)

GC Acquisition (Automated via Hermes)

Target: Mid-market commercial GCs (50–500 employees). Hermes identifies them via permit data, ENR rankings, and ABC/AGC member directories, then runs cold email sequences to GC Safety Directors.

No calls. No meetings. The email pitch + free portal signup is the entire acquisition motion. Hermes sends, follows up, handles replies, and routes signups to self-serve onboarding.

"You're already responsible when your subs get OSHA citations. You're already spending 10 hours a week chasing insurance certs by email. Kerf gives you a single dashboard. Your subs upload their own documents. You stop chasing. It costs you nothing. Set up takes 10 minutes — kerf.build/gc"
Critical Decision: GC Portal is FREE

The GC is the distribution channel, not the customer. Revenue comes from subs who upgrade. Zero friction at the seed node = maximum viral spread.

Sub Onboarding Flow

The invitation email is the single most important piece of copy:

  • Subject: "Action Required: [GC Name] — Safety Compliance Documentation"
  • Uses GC's logo prominently (sub opens it because they recognize their client)
  • Lists exact documents needed + time estimate ("5–10 minutes")
  • CTA is "Upload Your Documents" not "Sign Up for Kerf"

Conversion: forced compliance → willing adoption

  1. Weeks 1–4 (passive value): Compliance summaries, expiration alerts
  2. Months 2–6 (multi-GC value): Second GC invites same sub — docs auto-populate
  3. Months 3–12 (proactive value): Sub discovers doc generation, mock inspections. Upgrade moment.

Deliverability at Scale

  • AI-generated unique emails are essential. ESP fingerprinting detects templates at volume. Claude/GPT generates each email fresh for ~$0.50–2.00 per 10K emails
  • Validate all emails before sending (ZeroBounce/NeverBounce, $3–5 per 1K). Bounce rate must stay < 3%
  • Keep warmup running at all times — 30–50% of daily volume is warmup even at cruise speed
  • Budget for 5–10% domain burn per month. Keep 10–15% reserve domains warmed and ready
  • Plain text only. No images, max 1 link, short (50–120 words). Inbox placement: 45–55% at 5K/day volume
  • Hermes auto-pauses any mailbox/domain where bounce > 3% or spam > 0.1%

Agentic Discovery: How AI Finds and Recommends Kerf

94% of B2B buyers now use generative AI in purchasing decisions. If Kerf doesn't appear when a contractor asks ChatGPT "what safety software should I use?", it doesn't exist for that buyer.

The Opportunity

69% of sites block ClaudeBot. 62% block GPTBot. Only ~951 domains have published llms.txt. By allowing AI crawlers and publishing llms.txt, Kerf leapfrogs competitors who are hiding from AI.

1. Deploy /llms.txt (1 hour)
