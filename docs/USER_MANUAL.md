## 1. What is Kerf?

Kerf is an AI-powered safety compliance platform built for small-to-mid construction contractors (10-100 employees). It acts as your AI Safety Director, helping you:

- **Generate compliant safety documents** (SSSP, JHA, Fall Protection Plans, and more) in minutes instead of hours
- **Run site inspections** using checklists tailored to eight inspection types, with voice and photo modes coming soon
- **Track worker certifications** and get alerts before they expire
- **Deliver bilingual toolbox talks** (English and Spanish) with digital sign-off
- **Simulate OSHA inspections** to find gaps before a real inspector does
- **Automate prequalification** for ISNetworld, Avetta, and BROWZ
- **Report and track incidents** from the field
- **Support multiple jurisdictions** including the US, UK, Australia, and Canada

Kerf is designed for the job site. It works on phones, tablets, and desktops. The app includes a built-in English/Spanish language toggle so bilingual crews can switch the interface instantly.

## 2. Getting Started

**Why Use This:** Getting set up properly from the start means Kerf can tailor documents, inspections, and compliance checks to your specific trade, jurisdiction, and company size. A few minutes of setup saves hours of manual configuration later.

### Creating Your Account

1. Navigate to the Kerf landing page
2. Click **Try it free - no credit card** or **See what OSHA would find** to get started
3. On the sign-up page, choose one of the following:
   - **Google sign-up** for one-click account creation
   - **Email and password** to create a traditional account
4. You will start on the **Free tier** (no credit card required)

Alternatively, click **Try Demo - No Account Needed** on the login page to explore the app with sample data before committing to an account.

### Onboarding: Setting Up Your Company

After signing in for the first time, you will be directed to the onboarding page where Kerf collects essential information to configure your account:

1. **Country/Jurisdiction** - Select your operating jurisdiction: United States, United Kingdom, Australia, or Canada. Kerf tailors regulatory references, document templates, and compliance checks to match your jurisdiction's requirements.
2. **Company Name** - Your company or business name
3. **Trade Type** - Select from 12 available trades (e.g., General Contractor, Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC, Carpentry, Concrete, Roofing, Painting, Welding, Demolition, Excavation, Structural Steel)
4. **Phone** - Company phone number
5. **Address** - Company business address

Once onboarding is complete, you can further refine your company details from the Settings page at any time.

### Creating Your First Project

1. Click **Projects** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Project**
3. Enter the project details:
   - **Project Name** (required)
   - **Project Address** (required)
   - **Client/Owner**
   - **Project Type** (select from dropdown)
   - **Estimated Workers**
   - **Start and End Dates**
   - **Trade Types** - Select one or more from 14 checkboxes: General Contractor, Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC, Carpentry, Concrete, Roofing, Painting, Welding, Demolition, Excavation, Structural Steel, Masonry, Landscaping
   - **Safety section** - Special Hazards, Nearest Hospital, Emergency Contact Name and Phone
4. Click **Create Project**

Once created, all inspections, documents, toolbox talks, and incidents will be organized under this project.

### Navigating the App

The **sidebar** on the left organizes everything into five sections:

| Section | Contains |
|---------|----------|
| **Main** | Dashboard, Projects |
| **Safety** | Inspections, Documents, Incidents, Workers, Toolbox Talks, Mock OSHA Inspection, Equipment |
| **Compliance** | OSHA 300 Log, Regional Compliance, Prequalification, GC Portal, Environmental |
| **Reports** | Analytics |
| **Settings** | Settings, Billing |

**Important navigation note:** The sidebar links for Inspections, Incidents, and Toolbox Talks navigate to the Projects page, since these items are project-scoped. To access inspections, incidents, or toolbox talks for a specific project, first select the project and then use the tabs within the project detail page.

The header bar includes:

- **Current project name** displayed for context
- **EN/ES language toggle** to switch the entire interface between English and Spanish
- **Document usage counter** showing how many documents you have used on your current plan (e.g., "1/5 docs, Free tier") with an Upgrade button

On mobile devices, the sidebar is hidden behind a hamburger menu icon in the top-left corner.

## 3. Dashboard

Instead of checking multiple spreadsheets or binders, you can spot expiring certifications, review recent documents, and jump straight into creating an inspection or document -- all from one place.

The Dashboard is your at-a-glance compliance overview. It shows:

- **Active Projects** - Number of current projects
- **Inspections** - Total inspection count
- **Workers** - Number of workers in the system

## 4. Projects

**Why Use This:** Projects are the organizational backbone of Kerf. Every inspection, document, toolbox talk, hazard report, and incident belongs to a project. By organizing work this way, you always have a complete safety record for each job site, ready for audits, GC requests, or OSHA inquiries.

### Creating a Project

1. Go to **Projects** from the sidebar
2. Click **New Project**
3. Fill in:
   - **Project Name** (required) - e.g., "Riverside Office Build"
   - **Project Address** (required) - the job site location
   - **Client/Owner** - who the project is for
   - **Project Type** - select from the dropdown (commercial, residential, etc.)
   - **Estimated Workers** - expected crew size
   - **Start/End Dates** - project timeline
   - **Trade Types** - check all trades that apply from the 14 available options (General Contractor, Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC, Carpentry, Concrete, Roofing, Painting, Welding, Demolition, Excavation, Structural Steel, Masonry, Landscaping)
   - **Special Hazards** - any known site-specific hazards
   - **Nearest Hospital** - for emergency planning
   - **Emergency Contact Name and Phone** - the on-site emergency contact
4. Click **Create Project**

### Project List

The Projects page shows all your projects as cards with:

- Project name and status
- Worker count per project
- Quick action buttons per card: **Inspect**, **Document**, and a menu for additional actions
- Status filter dropdown to narrow the list by project status

### Project Detail Page

Click any project to see its detail page. At the top you will see the **compliance score ring** showing the project's overall compliance percentage (e.g., 78%).

**Action buttons** across the top provide quick access to:
- **New Inspection** - start an inspection for this project
- **New Toolbox Talk** - generate a safety talk
- **Photo Assessment** - capture and analyze site conditions
- **Report Incident** - file an incident report
- **Mock Inspection** - run a mock OSHA inspection scoped to this project

The project detail page is organized into **seven tabs**:

| Tab | What It Contains |
|-----|-----------------|
| **Overview** | Project details (type, dates, trades), safety and emergency info (special hazards, nearest hospital, emergency contact), Morning Brief section with "View Brief" button, and recent activity timeline |
| **Inspections** | All inspections conducted on this project, with count badge |
| **Toolbox Talks** | All safety talks created and delivered for this project |
| **Hazards** | Field-reported hazards on this project |
| **Incidents** | Any incidents that occurred on this project |
| **Documents** | Safety documents specific to this project (SSSP, JHA, etc.) |
| **Settings** | Project-level configuration |

### Morning Safety Brief

**Why Use This:** The Morning Brief gives your foreman or superintendent a data-driven safety briefing every morning, tailored to that day's conditions. Instead of guessing what to cover in a morning huddle, the brief highlights the most urgent risks -- whether that is extreme heat, an expiring certification, or an overdue inspection -- so the crew starts the day informed and protected.

Each morning, Kerf generates a brief tailored to your project. To view it, go to your project's Overview tab and click **View Brief**, or navigate directly to the Morning Brief page.

The brief includes:

- **Risk Score** (0-10) - An overall risk rating for the day (e.g., 7.2/10 "Elevated Risk"), calculated from weather, planned work, recent incidents, and certification gaps
- **Weather Conditions** - Current and forecasted conditions including temperature, cloud cover, wind speed, humidity, rain chance, and active weather advisories (e.g., Heat Advisory)
- **Alerts with Action Buttons** - Prioritized alerts, each with a direct action button:
  - Certification expiring (with **View Worker** button)
  - Weather advisories (with **View Heat Protocol** or relevant protocol button)
  - Daily Inspection overdue (with **Start Inspection** button)
  - Recommended Toolbox Talk topic (with **Generate Talk** button)
- **Recommended Toolbox Talk** - An AI-suggested topic based on the day's conditions and risks

## 5. Inspections

**Why Use This:** Regular inspections are not just an OSHA requirement -- they are how you catch hazards before they become injuries. Kerf makes inspections fast and structured so your foremen actually complete them, giving you a documented safety record that proves due diligence.

Kerf provides structured checklist-based inspections designed for speed in the field.

### Checklist Inspection

For structured, type-specific inspections:

1. From a project page, tap **New Inspection**
2. Choose the inspection type from eight available options:
   - Daily Site Inspection
   - Scaffold Inspection
   - Excavation/Trench Inspection
   - Fall Protection Inspection
   - Electrical Safety Inspection
   - Housekeeping Inspection
   - Equipment Inspection
   - Fire Safety Inspection
3. Fill in inspection details:
   - **Date** of the inspection
   - **Inspector Name**
   - **Weather** conditions
   - **Temperature**
   - **Wind** speed
   - **Workers on Site** count
4. Click **Start Checklist**
5. Work through the checklist, marking items as Pass, Fail, or N/A
6. Add notes and photos to any item
7. For failed items, the system prompts for a corrective action and deadline
8. Submit the inspection

### Voice Inspection (Coming Soon)

A voice-driven inspection mode is planned for a future release, allowing experienced foremen to walk a site and narrate observations. Kerf will transcribe the audio (English and Spanish supported) and extract hazards, corrective actions, and pass/fail items automatically.

### Photo Inspection (Coming Soon)

A dedicated photo inspection mode is planned for a future release, enabling AI-powered analysis of site photos to identify potential hazards.

### Viewing Past Inspections

- Go to a specific project and select the **Inspections** tab to see that project's inspections
- The sidebar **Inspections** link navigates to the Projects page, since inspections are project-scoped
- Each inspection shows: date, inspector, type, number of findings, and status
- Click any inspection to view the full report

**Why Use This:** Safety documents like SSSPs and JHAs are required on virtually every commercial construction project, but writing them from scratch takes hours and requires deep regulatory knowledge. Kerf generates compliant, project-specific documents in seconds using AI, so you spend your time reviewing and approving rather than writing from a blank page.

Kerf generates compliant safety documents using AI, customized to your company, project, and jurisdiction.

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|

Click on any document and select **Export as PDF** to download a formatted, print-ready version suitable for posting on site or submitting to GCs.

## 7. Workers & Certifications

**Why Use This:** Tracking worker certifications in spreadsheets is error-prone and time-consuming. One expired OSHA-10 card or lapsed fall protection certification can result in a citation or, worse, an unqualified worker in a dangerous situation. Kerf automates tracking and alerts so you never get caught off guard by an expiration.

### Adding Workers

1. Go to **Workers** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Worker**
3. Enter:
   - **First Name** (required)
   - **Last Name** (required)
   - **Role** - select from dropdown (laborer is the default)
   - **Trade** - select from dropdown (general is the default)
   - **Language Preference** - select from dropdown: English (en), Spanish (es), or both (en-es). This determines the language used for toolbox talks and other materials delivered to this worker.
   - **Phone**
   - **Email**
   - **Emergency Contact**
   - **Hire Date**
   - **Notes**
4. Click **Create Worker**

### Worker List

The Workers page displays all workers with:

- Name, role, trade, and language preference (shown as EN, ES, or EN-ES badges)
- Certification status at a glance
- **Search bar** to find workers quickly
- **Three filter dropdowns** to filter by role, trade, or status
- **Alert banners** at the top calling out expired and soon-to-expire certifications (e.g., "2 expired certifications" and "3 expiring in 30 days")

### Managing Certifications

Each worker can have multiple certifications tracked:

1. Open a worker's profile
2. Click **Add Certification**
3. Enter:
   - **Certification type** (OSHA-10, OSHA-30, Fall Protection, CPR/AED, Scaffold, Confined Space, Excavation, Forklift, Crane, Aerial Lift, HazCom, Silica, Electrical, Respirator Fit Test, and more)
   - **Issue date**
   - **Expiry date**
   - **Certificate number**
   - **Issuing body**
   - Upload a photo or scan of the certificate
4. Kerf automatically calculates the status:
   - **Valid** (green) - current and active
   - **Expiring Soon** (yellow) - expires within 30 days
   - **Expired** (red) - past expiry date

### Certification Alerts

Kerf sends alerts at 30, 14, 7, and 1 day before a certification expires. These appear:

- On the Dashboard in the **Certification Alerts** table and as a count in the status strip (**Cert Alerts**)
- In the **Morning Safety Brief** for projects where that worker is assigned
- As badge counts in the sidebar navigation

### Certification Matrix

Click the **Cert Matrix** button on the Workers page to open the Certification Matrix -- a grid view showing:

- **Rows**: All your workers (6 workers in the demo)
- **Columns**: 14 certification types (OSHA 10, OSHA 30, Fall Prot., CPR/AED, Scaffold, Conf. Space, Excavation, Forklift, Crane, Aerial Lift, HazCom, Silica, Electrical, Resp. Fit)
- **Cells**: Color-coded status:
  - **Green** - Valid
  - **Yellow** - Expiring Soon
  - **Red** - Expired
  - **Grey** - Missing / not assigned

This is the fastest way to see your entire crew's compliance status at a glance. Use the **Print** and **Export** buttons in the top-right to generate a version for posting in the site trailer or submitting to a GC.

## 8. Toolbox Talks

**Why Use This:** Toolbox talks are one of the simplest and most effective ways to reduce incidents on a job site, but creating relevant content every day is a burden. Kerf generates talks in both English and Spanish based on your actual site conditions and risks, so your morning huddles are always relevant and documented for compliance.

Toolbox talks are short (5-10 minute) safety discussions held before work begins. Kerf generates them in both English and Spanish simultaneously.

### Creating a Toolbox Talk

1. Navigate to a project and click **New Toolbox Talk** (from the project detail page action buttons)
2. Enter the details:
   - **Topic** (required) - Choose a topic, or let the AI suggest one based on current conditions
   - **Target Audience** - Select from dropdown (default: all workers)
   - **Duration** - Estimated duration in minutes
   - **Custom Points** - Optionally add site-specific talking points
3. Click **Generate Talk**
4. The AI generates the talk content in English and Spanish

Note: The sidebar link for **Toolbox Talks** navigates to the Projects page. To create or view toolbox talks, first select a project, then use the Toolbox Talks tab or the **New Toolbox Talk** action button.

### Delivering a Toolbox Talk

1. Open the talk and click **Deliver**
2. The delivery screen is optimized for mobile use in the field:
   - Large, readable text
   - Key points highlighted
   - Toggle between English and Spanish
3. After delivering the talk, each crew member signs on the screen:
   - Digital signature (finger or stylus)
   - Name and timestamp are captured automatically
4. Submit the completed talk

### Toolbox Talk Records

All delivered talks are automatically logged with:

- Topic and content
- Date and time
- Attendee list with signatures
- Project association
- Who delivered it

These records satisfy OSHA training documentation requirements and are available for audits, GC requests, and prequalification packages.

## 9. Incidents

**Why Use This:** The faster you document an incident, the more accurate the record. Waiting until the end of the day -- or worse, the end of the week -- means critical details are lost. Kerf structures incident reporting so it happens on site, in the moment, with all the information needed for OSHA compliance and root cause analysis.

### Reporting an Incident

From a project page, tap **Report Incident** to open the incident form. Fill in:

- **Date** - When the incident occurred
- **Time** - Time of the incident
- **Location** - Where on the site it happened
- **Severity** - Select from dropdown (minor, moderate, serious, etc.)
- **Description** - What happened
- **Persons Involved** - Who was involved
- **Witnesses** - Who saw it happen
- **Immediate Actions Taken** - What was done right away

Click **Report Incident** to submit.

Note: The sidebar link for **Incidents** navigates to the Projects page. To report or view incidents, first select a project, then use the Incidents tab or the **Report Incident** action button.

**Voice Reporting (Coming Soon):** A voice-driven incident reporting mode is planned for a future release. You will be able to narrate what happened and the AI will transcribe and structure the report automatically, extracting date, time, location, persons involved, injury type, root cause, and corrective action recommendations.

### OSHA Classification

Kerf automatically classifies incidents:

- **OSHA Recordable?** - Does this go on the OSHA 300 log?
- **OSHA Reportable?** - Does this require reporting to OSHA within 8/24 hours? (fatality, hospitalization, amputation, loss of eye)

### Incident Tracking

Each incident follows a workflow:

1. **Reported** - Initial report filed
2. **Investigating** - Root cause analysis underway
3. **Corrective Actions** - Fixes being implemented
4. **Closed** - Investigation complete, actions verified

View all incidents for a project from the project's **Incidents** tab.

## 10. Mock OSHA Inspection

**Why Use This:** An actual OSHA inspection can result in thousands of dollars in fines and project shutdowns. The Mock Inspection lets you find and fix gaps proactively, so when a real inspector shows up, you are ready. It also gives you a documented record of proactive compliance efforts, which can reduce penalties if citations are issued.

The Mock OSHA Inspection simulates what would happen if an OSHA compliance officer walked onto your site today.

### Running a Mock Inspection

1. Go to **Mock OSHA Inspection** in the sidebar (under Safety)
2. Select the **scope** from the dropdown (company-wide or a specific project)
3. Click **Run Mock Inspection**
4. Kerf reviews your entire compliance posture:
   - Are all required safety documents in place and current?
   - Are worker certifications up to date?
   - Are inspections being conducted at the required frequency?
   - Are incidents properly documented and closed?
   - Are there gaps in required safety programs?
   - Are project-specific plans adequate for the work being performed?

### Understanding Results

The inspection returns a summary and detailed findings:

**Summary statistics** show how many documents, training records, and inspections were reviewed (e.g., 5 documents, 18 training records, 6 inspections reviewed).

For each finding:
- **Finding ID** and **severity** (Critical, High, Medium, Low, Info)
- **OSHA standard reference** (e.g., 29 CFR 1926.502 - Fall Protection)
- **Observed condition** - what the inspector would see
- **Citation language** - how OSHA would write it up
- **Corrective action** - what you need to do to fix it
- **Estimated penalty range** - what the fine could be ($0 to $16,000+)
- **Auto-fix available?** - whether Kerf can generate the missing document for you

### Readiness Score

You receive an overall score:
- **Score**: 0-100
- **Grade**: A through F
- **A (90-100)**: Inspection-ready
- **B (80-89)**: Minor gaps, easily fixable
- **C (70-79)**: Notable gaps, needs attention
- **D (60-69)**: Significant deficiencies
- **F (below 60)**: High risk of citations

Past inspection results are saved and displayed on the Mock Inspection page for comparison over time (e.g., Score 62, Grade C, 9 findings, 2 critical).

### Exporting Results

Click **Export as PDF** to download the full mock inspection report. This is useful for:
- Sharing with your safety committee
- Presenting to ownership
- Documenting your proactive compliance efforts (this can reduce penalties if OSHA does visit)

## 12. OSHA Log

**Why Use This:** Maintaining an accurate OSHA 300 Log is a federal requirement, and errors can result in penalties even if the underlying incidents were handled properly. Kerf keeps your log organized, auto-generates the 300A Annual Summary, and reminds you about the annual posting requirement so you stay compliant without the paperwork headache.

Kerf manages your OSHA 300 Log and 300A Annual Summary.

### What is the OSHA 300 Log?

Every employer with 11 or more employees must maintain an OSHA Form 300 -- a log of all work-related injuries and illnesses.

### How Kerf Helps

- **Auto-population**: When you report an incident classified as OSHA-recordable, it automatically creates an entry on the 300 log
- **Entry management**: View, edit, and manage all log entries for the current year
- **Annual summary**: Automatically generates the OSHA 300A summary at year-end
- **Multi-year records**: Access logs from previous years using the year selector (OSHA requires 5-year retention)
- **Posting reminder**: A warning banner reminds you about the 300A annual posting requirement

### Navigating the OSHA Log

The OSHA Log page has **three tabs**:

| Tab | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **300 Log** | The main log of recordable injuries and illnesses for the selected year |
| **300A Summary** | The annual summary form required to be posted in the workplace |
| **Incidence Rates** | Calculated TRIR, DART, and other rates for benchmarking |

Use the **year selector** to switch between years. Click **Add Entry** to manually add a recordable case. Each entry includes a **Delete** button if you need to remove an erroneous record.

### Managing Entries

Each entry includes:
- Employee name
- Date of injury/illness
- Where the event occurred
- Description of injury/illness
- Classification (death, days away, restricted duty, other recordable)
- Number of days away from work / days of restricted duty

## 13. Regional Compliance

**Why Use This:** Safety regulations vary significantly by jurisdiction. What is compliant in Texas may not be compliant in California, and UK or Australian requirements differ substantially from US federal OSHA. Regional Compliance ensures you are meeting the specific requirements for wherever you operate, not just the baseline federal standards.

Kerf tracks jurisdiction-specific requirements beyond the baseline standards for your selected country. This feature adapts based on the jurisdiction you selected during onboarding (US, UK, Australia, or Canada).

### How It Works

1. Go to **Regional Compliance** in the sidebar (listed under Compliance)
2. Select your specific state, province, or region from the dropdown
3. Kerf shows:
   - Region-specific safety requirements that go beyond the national baseline
   - Required filings and their deadlines
   - Region-specific training requirements
   - Differences from national standards

### US State Plan States

If you operate in a US "State Plan" state (like California, Washington, or Michigan), those states have their own OSHA programs with additional requirements beyond federal OSHA. Kerf covers these differences.

### Multi-Jurisdiction Support

Kerf supports contractors operating in multiple jurisdictions. The platform adapts its regulatory references, document templates, and compliance checks based on your selected jurisdiction:

- **United States** - Federal OSHA and state-specific requirements
- **United Kingdom** - HSE (Health and Safety Executive) standards
- **Australia** - Safe Work Australia and state/territory WHS requirements
- **Canada** - Provincial OHS requirements

## 14. Prequalification

**Why Use This:** Prequalification is one of the most time-consuming administrative tasks for subcontractors. GCs and owner-operators require extensive safety documentation before you can bid on or start a project. Kerf scans your existing data, identifies gaps, and can generate missing documents -- turning a multi-day exercise into a matter of hours.

Automate the tedious process of filling out prequalification packages for ISNetworld, Avetta, and BROWZ.

### How It Works

1. Go to **Prequalification** in the sidebar
2. Select the **platform** from the dropdown (ISNetworld, Avetta, or BROWZ)
3. Enter the **client name** you are prequalifying for
4. Kerf scans your existing data and shows an **Overall Readiness** score (e.g., 72%)
5. Documents are organized by category with item counts:
   - **Safety Programs** (e.g., 7/10 items ready)
   - **OSHA Records** (e.g., 2/3 items ready)

Kerf helps you generate and maintain these programs proactively, with proper regulatory references so you can demonstrate compliance.

Track environmental compliance requirements including exposure monitoring and control programs.

### Programs

The Environmental page is organized into **three tabs**:

| Tab | Purpose |
|-----|---------|

| **Exposure Monitoring** | Logged exposure monitoring records |
| **SWPPP** | Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan management |

The Programs tab shows:

**Available Programs** - Programs you can generate as needed:
- Lead exposure control
