## 1. What is SafetyForge?

SafetyForge is an AI-powered safety compliance platform built for small-to-mid construction contractors (10-100 employees). It acts as your AI Safety Director, helping you:

- **Generate OSHA-compliant safety documents** (SSSP, JHA, Fall Protection Plans, and more) in minutes instead of hours
- **Run site inspections** using voice, photos, or traditional checklists
- **Track worker certifications** and get alerts before they expire
- **Deliver bilingual toolbox talks** (English and Spanish) with digital sign-off
- **Simulate OSHA inspections** to find gaps before a real inspector does
- **Automate prequalification** for ISNetworld, Avetta, and BROWZ
- **Report and track incidents** from the field using voice

SafetyForge is designed for the job site. It works on phones, tablets, and desktops. Foremen can complete a full inspection in under 2 minutes using voice input.

## 2. Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. Navigate to the SafetyForge signup page
2. Enter your name, email address, and password
3. Verify your email address via the confirmation link
4. You will start with a **14-day free trial** of the Professional tier (no credit card required)

### Setting Up Your Company

After signing in for the first time:

1. Go to **Settings** in the sidebar
2. Fill in your company profile:
   - Company name
   - Trade/specialty (e.g., electrical, plumbing, general contracting)
   - Number of employees
   - Address and contact information
   - EMR (Experience Modification Rate) if known
3. **Invite your team** by entering their email addresses and assigning roles (see [User Roles](#19-user-roles--permissions))

### Creating Your First Project

1. Click **Projects** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Project**
3. Enter the project details:
   - Project name and address
   - General contractor (if applicable)
   - Start and end dates
   - Scope of work
4. Once created, all inspections, documents, toolbox talks, and incidents will be organized under this project

### Navigating the App

The **sidebar** on the left organizes everything into five sections:

| Section | Contains |
|---------|----------|
| **Main** | Dashboard, Projects |
| **Safety** | Inspections, Documents, Incidents, Workers, Toolbox Talks, Mock Inspection, Equipment |
| **Compliance** | OSHA Log, State Compliance, Prequalification, GC Portal, Environmental |
| **Reports** | Analytics |
| **Settings** | Settings, Billing |

On mobile devices, the sidebar is hidden behind a hamburger menu icon in the top-left corner.

## 4. Projects

Projects are the organizational backbone of SafetyForge. Every inspection, document, toolbox talk, and incident belongs to a project.

### Creating a Project

1. Go to **Projects** from the sidebar
2. Click **New Project**
3. Fill in:
   - **Project name** (e.g., "Riverside Office Build")
   - **Address** and site location
   - **General contractor** name
   - **Start/end dates**
   - **Trade scope** (what your crew is doing on this job)
4. Click **Create Project**

### Project Detail Page

Click any project to see its detail page, which includes:

- **Project overview** - Address, dates, scope, GC info
- **Morning Safety Brief** - AI-generated daily risk assessment (see below)
- **Inspections** - All inspections for this project
- **Toolbox Talks** - Talks scheduled or delivered on this project
- **Hazard Reports** - Field-reported hazards
- **Incidents** - Any incidents that occurred on this project
- **Documents** - Safety documents specific to this project (SSSP, JHA, etc.)

### Morning Safety Brief

Each morning, SafetyForge generates a brief tailored to your project. It includes:

- **Weather forecast** - Temperature, conditions, and weather alerts for the site
- **Risk score** (0-10) - Based on weather, planned work, recent incidents, and certification gaps
- **Crew alerts** - Certifications expiring today or this week
- **Recommended toolbox talk topic** - AI-suggested based on the day's planned work and risks
- **Action items** - 3-5 things to address before the crew starts

To view the morning brief, go to your project and click **Morning Brief**.

## 5. Inspections

SafetyForge supports three ways to conduct inspections, all designed for speed in the field.

### Voice Inspection

The fastest method for experienced foremen:

1. From a project page, tap **New Inspection**
2. Select **Voice** mode
3. Press and hold the record button
4. Walk the site and narrate what you see:
   > "Trench on grid B, shoring looks good. East wall forms need guardrails added before second pour. PPE looks compliant on all crew..."
5. Release when done. SafetyForge transcribes your audio (English and Spanish supported)
6. The AI extracts:
   - Hazards identified
   - Corrective actions needed
   - Location references
   - Items that pass/fail
7. Review the structured report, attach photos, and submit

### Checklist Inspection

For structured, type-specific inspections:

1. From a project page, tap **New Inspection**
2. Choose the inspection type:
   - Daily Site Inspection
   - Scaffold Inspection
   - Excavation/Trench Inspection
   - Electrical Safety Inspection
   - Fall Protection Inspection
   - Housekeeping Inspection
   - Equipment Inspection
   - Fire Safety Inspection
3. Work through the checklist, marking items as Pass, Fail, or N/A
4. Add notes and photos to any item
5. For failed items, the system prompts for a corrective action and deadline
6. Submit the inspection

### Photo Inspection

Capture visual evidence alongside your inspection:

1. Take photos of conditions on site
2. Attach them to inspection items
3. The AI can analyze photos to suggest potential hazards

### Viewing Past Inspections

- Go to **Inspections** in the sidebar to see all inspections across all projects
- Or go to a specific project to see only that project's inspections
- Each inspection shows: date, inspector, type, number of findings, and status
- Click any inspection to view the full report

## 6. Documents

SafetyForge generates OSHA-compliant safety documents using AI, customized to your company and project.

### Document Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **SSSP** | Site-Specific Safety Plan - comprehensive safety plan for a project |
| **JHA** | Job Hazard Analysis - step-by-step hazard identification for specific tasks |
| **Fall Protection Plan** | Project-specific fall protection procedures |
| **Toolbox Talk** | Short safety topic for crew meetings |
| **Incident Report** | Structured incident documentation |
| **Hazard Communication** | Chemical safety and SDS management plan |

### Generating a Document

1. Go to **Documents** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Document**
3. Select the document type
4. Provide context:
   - Which project is this for?
   - What trade or scope of work?
   - Any specific hazards or site conditions to address?
5. Click **Generate**
6. The AI creates a complete, OSHA-compliant document in seconds
7. Review and edit the content as needed
8. Set the status:
   - **Draft** - still being worked on
   - **Final** - approved and ready for use
   - **Archived** - no longer active

### Document Library

The Documents page shows all your company's safety documents with:

- Document title and type
- Associated project
- Current status (Draft, Final, Archived)
- Last updated date
- Quick search and filter

### Exporting Documents

Click on any document and select **Export as PDF** to download a formatted, print-ready version suitable for posting on site or submitting to GCs.

### Templates

Go to **Templates** (accessible from the Documents page) to browse pre-built document templates. These give you a starting point that the AI then customizes for your specific company and project.

## 7. Workers & Certifications

### Adding Workers

1. Go to **Workers** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Worker**
3. Enter:
   - Full name
   - Job title / role
   - Trade (electrician, ironworker, laborer, etc.)
   - Contact information (phone, email)
   - Emergency contact
4. Click **Create Worker**

### Managing Certifications

Each worker can have multiple certifications tracked:

1. Open a worker's profile
2. Click **Add Certification**
3. Enter:
   - **Certification type** (OSHA-10, OSHA-30, Fall Protection, Scaffold Competent Person, Confined Space, First Aid/CPR, Rigging, Crane Operator, etc.)
   - **Issue date**
   - **Expiry date**
   - **Certificate number**
   - **Issuing body**
   - Upload a photo or scan of the certificate
4. SafetyForge automatically calculates the status:
   - **Valid** (green) - current and active
   - **Expiring Soon** (yellow) - expires within 30 days
   - **Expired** (red) - past expiry date

### Certification Alerts

SafetyForge sends alerts at 30, 14, 7, and 1 day before a certification expires. These appear:

- On the Dashboard under Certification Alerts
- In the Morning Safety Brief for projects where that worker is assigned
- As badge counts in the sidebar navigation

### Certification Matrix

Go to **Workers > Certification Matrix** for a grid view showing:

- Rows: all your workers
- Columns: all certification types
- Cells: color-coded status (green = valid, yellow = expiring, red = expired, grey = not required)

This is the fastest way to see your entire crew's compliance status at a glance.

## 8. Toolbox Talks

Toolbox talks are short (5-10 minute) safety discussions held before work begins. SafetyForge generates them in both English and Spanish simultaneously.

### Creating a Toolbox Talk

1. Navigate to a project and click **New Toolbox Talk**
2. Choose a topic, or let the AI suggest one based on:
   - The day's planned work
   - Recent incidents or near-misses
   - Weather conditions
   - Common hazards for your trade
3. Optionally customize with site-specific talking points
4. The AI generates the talk content in English and Spanish

### Delivering a Toolbox Talk

1. Open the talk and click **Deliver**
2. The delivery screen is optimized for mobile use in the field:
   - Large, readable text
   - Key points highlighted
   - Toggle between English and Spanish
3. After delivering the talk, each crew member signs on the screen:
   - Digital signature (finger or stylus)
   - Name and timestamp are captured automatically
4. Submit the completed talk

### Toolbox Talk Records

All delivered talks are automatically logged with:

- Topic and content
- Date and time
- Attendee list with signatures
- Project association
- Who delivered it

These records satisfy OSHA training documentation requirements and are available for audits, GC requests, and prequalification packages.

## 9. Incidents

### Reporting an Incident

SafetyForge makes incident reporting fast so it happens on site, not hours later from memory.

**Voice Reporting (recommended):**

1. From a project page, tap **Report Incident**
2. Press and hold the voice button
3. Narrate what happened:
   > "Luis stepped on a nail at 1:45 PM, Building B north side. First aid was applied on site. Nail was in a scrap pile that wasn't cleaned up from yesterday."
4. The AI transcribes and structures the report:
   - Date, time, and location
   - Person(s) involved
   - Injury type and severity
   - Root cause identification
   - Corrective action recommendation
5. Review, add photos, and submit

**Manual Reporting:**

Fill in the incident form with:

- Date, time, and location
- Description of what happened
- Person(s) involved and their injuries (if any)
- Witnesses
- Immediate actions taken
- Photos or evidence

### OSHA Classification

SafetyForge automatically classifies incidents:

- **OSHA Recordable?** - Does this go on the OSHA 300 log?
- **OSHA Reportable?** - Does this require reporting to OSHA within 8/24 hours? (fatality, hospitalization, amputation, loss of eye)

### Incident Tracking

Each incident follows a workflow:

1. **Reported** - Initial report filed
2. **Investigating** - Root cause analysis underway
3. **Corrective Actions** - Fixes being implemented
4. **Closed** - Investigation complete, actions verified

View all incidents from the **Incidents** page in the sidebar, or from within a specific project.

## 10. Mock OSHA Inspection

The Mock OSHA Inspection is one of SafetyForge's most powerful features. It simulates what would happen if an OSHA compliance officer walked onto your site today.

### Running a Mock Inspection

1. Go to **Mock Inspection** in the sidebar
2. Click **Run Inspection**
3. SafetyForge reviews your entire compliance posture:
   - Are all required safety documents in place and current?
   - Are worker certifications up to date?
   - Are inspections being conducted at the required frequency?
   - Are incidents properly documented and closed?
   - Are there gaps in required safety programs?
   - Are project-specific plans adequate for the work being performed?

### Understanding Results

The inspection returns findings formatted exactly like real OSHA citations:

For each finding:
- **Finding ID** and **severity** (Critical, High, Medium, Low, Info)
- **OSHA standard reference** (e.g., 29 CFR 1926.502 - Fall Protection)
- **Observed condition** - what the inspector would see
- **Citation language** - how OSHA would write it up
- **Corrective action** - what you need to do to fix it
- **Estimated penalty range** - what the fine could be ($0 to $16,000+)
- **Auto-fix available?** - whether SafetyForge can generate the missing document for you

### Readiness Score

You receive an overall score:
- **Score**: 0-100
- **Grade**: A through F
- **A (90-100)**: Inspection-ready
- **B (80-89)**: Minor gaps, easily fixable
- **C (70-79)**: Notable gaps, needs attention
- **D (60-69)**: Significant deficiencies
- **F (below 60)**: High risk of citations

### Exporting Results

Click **Export as PDF** to download the full mock inspection report. This is useful for:
- Sharing with your safety committee
- Presenting to ownership
- Documenting your proactive compliance efforts (this can reduce penalties if OSHA does visit)

## 12. OSHA Log

SafetyForge manages your OSHA 300 Log and 300A Annual Summary.

### What is the OSHA 300 Log?

Every employer with 11 or more employees must maintain an OSHA Form 300 - a log of all work-related injuries and illnesses.

### How SafetyForge Helps

- **Auto-population**: When you report an incident classified as OSHA-recordable, it automatically creates an entry on the 300 log
- **Entry management**: View, edit, and manage all log entries for the current year
- **Annual summary**: Automatically generates the OSHA 300A summary at year-end
- **Multi-year records**: Access logs from previous years (OSHA requires 5-year retention)

### Managing Entries

Each entry includes:
- Employee name
- Date of injury/illness
- Where the event occurred
- Description of injury/illness
- Classification (death, days away, restricted duty, other recordable)
- Number of days away from work / days of restricted duty

## 13. State Compliance

OSHA requirements vary by state. SafetyForge tracks state-specific requirements beyond federal standards.

### How It Works

1. Go to **State Compliance** in the sidebar
2. Your company's state is auto-detected from your profile
3. SafetyForge shows:
   - State-specific safety requirements that go beyond federal OSHA
   - Required state filings and their deadlines
   - State-specific training requirements
   - Differences from federal standards

### State Plan States

If you operate in a "State Plan" state (like California, Washington, or Michigan), those states have their own OSHA programs with additional requirements. SafetyForge covers these differences.

## 14. Prequalification

Automate the tedious process of filling out prequalification packages for ISNetworld, Avetta, and BROWZ.

### How It Works

1. Go to **Prequalification** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Package**
3. Select the platform: ISNetworld, Avetta, or BROWZ
4. SafetyForge scans your existing data and identifies what's needed:
   - **Ready** (green) - Document exists and is current
   - **Outdated** (yellow) - Document exists but needs updating
   - **Missing** (red) - Needs to be created
5. For missing or outdated items, click **Generate** to have the AI create the document
6. Review and approve each document
7. Export the complete package

### What's Included

A typical prequalification package requires:
- Company profile and insurance certificates
- Safety programs (SSSP, Fall Protection, Hazcom, etc.)
- Training records and certifications
- OSHA 300 logs and incident history
- EMR (Experience Modification Rate) documentation
- Questionnaire responses

SafetyForge pre-fills questionnaires using information already in your account, saving hours of repetitive data entry.

## 15. GC Portal

The GC Portal enables transparency between general contractors and subcontractors.

### For Subcontractors

If a GC invites you to share your safety data:

1. You will receive an invitation notification
2. Go to **GC Portal** in the sidebar
3. Review the invitation and what data the GC is requesting access to
4. Accept or decline the relationship
5. Once accepted, the GC can view (read-only):
   - Your safety documents
   - Daily inspection logs
   - Worker training records
   - Incident reports (if you grant permission)

### For General Contractors

If you are a GC managing subcontractors:

1. Go to **GC Portal**
2. Invite subcontractors by email
3. View a real-time compliance dashboard for each sub
4. Monitor:
   - Are they conducting daily inspections?
   - Are their workers' certifications current?
   - Are their safety documents up to date?
   - What's their incident history?

## 16. Environmental

Track environmental compliance requirements including exposure monitoring and control programs.

### Monitoring Types

- **Silica** - Respirable crystalline silica (Table 1 compliance)
- **Lead** - Lead exposure monitoring
- **Asbestos** - Asbestos exposure and abatement
- **Noise** - Occupational noise exposure
- **Stormwater** - SWPPP compliance
- **Dust Control** - Fugitive dust monitoring
- **Hazmat** - Hazardous materials handling

### Logging Exposure Records

For each monitoring event, record:
- Worker name
- Monitoring date and location
- Sample type (personal, area, bulk)
- Result value
- Action level threshold
- PEL (Permissible Exposure Limit) threshold

SafetyForge automatically flags any results that exceed action levels or PELs and recommends additional controls.

## 19. User Roles & Permissions

When inviting team members, assign one of these roles:

| Role | Can Do | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Owner** | Everything, including billing and account deletion | Company principal or founder |
| **Admin** | Everything except billing changes | Operations manager, safety director |

## 20. Mobile Usage

SafetyForge is built mobile-first. Construction workers use phones and tablets on site, so every feature works on small screens.

### Tips for Field Use

- **Tap the hamburger menu** (top-left) to open the sidebar navigation on mobile
- **Use voice input** for inspections and incident reports instead of typing
- **Photos attach directly** from your device camera
- **Toolbox talk delivery** mode uses large, readable text optimized for outdoor viewing
- **Digital signatures** work with finger touch or stylus
- **Offline capability** - inspections started offline will sync when you regain connectivity

### Recommended Devices

SafetyForge works on any modern browser. For best field experience:
- iPhone or Android phone (any model from the last 3 years)
- iPad or Android tablet (ideal for toolbox talk delivery and certification matrix)
- Rugged cases recommended for construction site use
