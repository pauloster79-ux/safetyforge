## 1. Executive Summary

Kerf is evolving from an AI Safety Director into **the construction operations platform for small-to-mid contractors** — one tool that replaces six. Starting with safety compliance (Horizon 1), expanding to daily logs, time tracking, and quality inspections (Horizon 2), then project coordination (Horizon 3), and finally predictive intelligence (Horizon 4).

This expansion fundamentally changes our pricing strategy. We are no longer competing only with safety tools ($16-29/user/month). We are competing with the combined cost of safety software + daily log tools + time trackers + quality inspection apps + project coordination tools — a stack that costs contractors $400-1,200/month in per-user fees across multiple vendors, or $30,000+/year for Procore.

Our pricing must reflect this expanded value while remaining accessible to the 750,000 small contractors who are our target.

### Current Pricing

| Tier | Monthly | Projects | Doc Limit | Key Differentiator |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Free | $0 | 1 | 3/mo | Trial & demonstration |
| Starter | $99 | 2 | 10/mo | Mock inspections, morning briefs |
| Professional | $299 | 8 | Unlimited | AI voice, photo hazard, prequal |
| Business | $599 | 20 | Unlimited | GC portal, EMR modeling |
| Enterprise | Custom | Unlimited | Unlimited | SSO, API, white-label |

### Key Recommendations

1. **Reframe tiers around operational scope, not just safety features.** As Horizon 2 features ship, each tier should unlock operational modules (daily logs, time tracking, quality) — not just safety capabilities.
2. **Increase document limits** on Free (3 to 5) and Starter (10 to 25) to improve conversion.
3. **Reduce Business tier** from $599 to $499 to close the pricing gap.
4. **Increase annual discount** from 20% to 25%.
5. **Plan for Horizon 2 pricing** — daily logs, time tracking, and quality inspections dramatically increase the value delivered, justifying current price points even without a price increase.

## 2. Market Analysis

### Market Size and Growth

The global EHS software market reached **$2.8B in 2025** and is projected to grow at **11.7% CAGR to $7.6B by 2034**. But Kerf's TAM now extends beyond EHS into construction operations — daily logs, time tracking, quality management, and project coordination. The combined construction technology market for small contractors is significantly larger.

### The "One Tool, Not Six" Opportunity

The vision document identifies that construction workers average **6.2 separate technology products**. The real competitive landscape is the combined spend:

| Tool Category | Typical Cost (25-person crew) | Kerf Replaces? |
|--------------|------------------------------|----------------|
| Safety compliance | $300-600/mo (per-user) | Horizon 1 (now) |
| Daily log software | $150-400/mo (Raken, etc.) | Horizon 2 |
| Time tracking | $100-300/mo (ClockShark, Busybusy) | Horizon 2 |
| Quality inspections | $200-500/mo (often bundled) | Horizon 2 |
| Sub compliance | $200-400/mo (various) | Horizon 2-3 |
| Project coordination | $500-2,500/mo (Procore, Buildertrend) | Horizon 3 |
| **Total stack cost** | **$1,450-4,800/mo** | **$99-499/mo** |

Kerf at $299/month (Professional) replaces $1,450-4,800/month in fragmented tools. This is not a marginal savings — it is an order-of-magnitude reduction. The pricing headroom is enormous.

### Pricing Trends (2025-2026)

- **SaaS prices rising:** 9-25% increases across the industry, with 50% planning further increases
- **Annual discounts increasing:** Industry average now 28% (up from 15% in 2022)
- **AI features drive premium pricing:** 28% of EHS functions already use AI; 53% plan 10%+ budget increases
- **Platform consolidation is the trend:** Buyers prefer fewer tools, even at higher per-tool prices

### Target Customer Economics

Small-to-mid specialty contractors spend:
- **$86K-235K/year** on safety compliance alone
- **$52K/year** on bad time tracking (50-person crew estimate from vision doc)
- **$5K-15K/year** on safety consultant engagements
- **$60K-90K/year** for a full-time safety director

Kerf at $99-499/month ($1,188-5,988/year) represents **under 3% of total operational overhead** — a low-risk purchase that replaces multiple tools and consultant fees.

## 4. Current Pricing Assessment

### Strengths

1. **Per-project model is rare and powerful.** Only ~20% of competitors avoid per-user pricing. For a 25-person crew, Kerf saves 25-50% vs per-seat competitors on safety alone — and the gap widens dramatically as Horizon 2 features ship.

2. **Unlimited users is the key differentiator.** The vision document is explicit: "Unlimited users on every tier. This is non-negotiable." Per-user pricing kills field adoption. Carlos the laborer, who needs to report hazards in Spanish, should never be a line item debate.

3. **$99 entry is an impulse purchase.** Less than one day of a safety consultant. Low enough that a foreman can put it on a company card without approval.

4. **AI features bundled, not add-on.** While competitors charge $25-50/user/mo for AI, Kerf includes voice input, photo hazard, video walkthrough, and AI document generation in Professional+.

5. **Multi-jurisdiction compliance is unique.** No competitor addresses multiple state regulatory frameworks.

6. **Platform expansion creates massive pricing headroom.** When Kerf replaces 3-6 tools, the $299/month price becomes absurdly good value — even without a price increase.

### Weaknesses

1. **$299 to $599 jump is steep (2x).** Most SaaS products maintain 1.5-1.8x tier multipliers.

2. **Document limits feel restrictive.** Free (3/mo) and Starter (10/mo) are below competitor free tiers.

3. **Tier names don't reflect operational breadth.** As Horizon 2 ships, "Starter" and "Professional" don't communicate that the product includes daily logs, time tracking, and quality management.

4. **Annual discount (20%) below rising SaaS average (28%).**

5. **No pricing strategy for Horizon 2 features.** As daily logs, time tracking, and quality ship, we need to decide: include in existing tiers (absorb-and-retain) or gate behind upgrades (monetise-and-upsell)?

## 5. Strategic Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Horizon 2 Feature Inclusion Strategy

This is the most important pricing decision for the next 12 months. Two options:

**Option A: Include in existing tiers (Recommended)**

Daily logs, time tracking, and quality inspections are included at the same tier where their prerequisite safety features already exist. No price increase.

| Feature | Available At | Rationale |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Daily logs | Professional+ | Auto-populates from safety walk data; same workflow |
| Time tracking | Starter+ | GPS clock-in is lightweight; drives daily engagement |
| Quality inspections | Professional+ | Same inspection engine as safety; same walk |
| Equipment management | Business+ | Extends existing equipment inspection workflow |
| Sub compliance (expanded) | Business+ | Extends existing GC portal |

**Why this is right:**
- The vision says "one tool, not six" — charging separately for each module contradicts the core promise
- The product principle says features should integrate, not silo — pricing should match
- Including Horizon 2 features justifies the current price points without raising prices — $299/mo for safety + daily logs + time tracking + quality is extraordinary value
- Drives the "app consolidation rate" metric (target: 2+ tools replaced)
- Drives the "features per customer" metric (target: 3.5+ by month 18)
- Reduces churn because multi-feature users never leave
- Creates pricing headroom for a future increase once the platform is proven

**Option B: Module-based add-ons (Not recommended)**

Each Horizon 2 feature is a $49-99/month add-on. This maximises short-term ARPU but contradicts the "one tool" positioning, creates adoption friction, and makes the pricing page look like every enterprise tool Kerf is trying to replace.

### Recommendation 2: Increase Document Limits

| Tier | Current | Proposed | Rationale |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Free | 3/mo | 5/mo | One doc/week — enough to experience value |
| Starter | 10/mo | 25/mo | Covers weekly cadence without limit anxiety |

**Risk:** Low. Primary conversion trigger is project count, not document count.

### Recommendation 3: Reduce Business Tier to $499/month

| | Current | Proposed |
|---|---------|----------|
| Business price | $599/mo | $499/mo |
| Pro-to-Business multiplier | 2.0x | 1.67x |
| Annual (25% off) | $5,390 | $4,491 |

**Rationale:** Brings multiplier to SaaS norms (1.5-1.8x). $499 is under the $500 threshold. With Horizon 2 features included, $499/mo for safety + daily logs + time + quality + GC portal + EMR modeling is still extraordinary value vs the $1,450-4,800/mo tool stack it replaces.

### Recommendation 4: Increase Annual Discount to 25%

| | Current (20%) | Proposed (25%) |
|---|---|---|
| Starter annual | $950/yr | $891/yr ($74.25/mo) |
| Professional annual | $2,868/yr | $2,691/yr ($224.25/mo) |
| Business annual | $5,752/yr | $4,491/yr ($374.25/mo) |

### Recommendation 5: Plan for Horizon 3 Price Increase (Month 18+)

When RFIs, scheduling, and change order tracking ship (Horizon 3), Kerf becomes a genuine Procore alternative for $5-50M GCs. At that point:

- **Evaluate raising Professional to $349/mo and Business to $549/mo** — still 1/10th the cost of Procore
- **Grandfather existing customers** at their current rate for 12 months
- **The narrative:** "We added $500/month of value and raised the price $50/month"

This is a future decision, not a current one. Do not raise prices until Horizon 3 features are shipping and proven.

## 6. Pricing Philosophy

### Why Per-Project, Not Per-User

The per-project model is a deliberate strategic choice that becomes even more important as Kerf expands:

1. **Aligns with customer mental model.** Contractors think in projects, not users.

2. **Eliminates adoption friction across ALL features.** Per-user pricing doesn't just limit safety adoption — it would limit daily log adoption, time tracking adoption, quality inspection adoption. Every module Kerf adds is a module where unlimited users matters. If Carlos can't clock in because he's not a paid seat, the time tracking feature fails.

3. **Creates compounding network effects.** More users per account = more safety data + more time data + more quality data + more daily log data. The AI gets smarter faster. The predictive models improve. The morning brief becomes more valuable.

4. **Competitive moat widens with each horizon.** A 50-person crew on Kerf Professional pays $299/mo. The same crew using SafetyCulture ($600) + ClockShark ($375) + Raken ($300) pays $1,275/mo. The savings gap gets wider as Kerf replaces more tools.

5. **"One tool, not six" only works if there's one price.** The moment you charge per-module or per-user, you've recreated the fragmented pricing that makes contractors use six separate tools in the first place.

### Value Metric Alignment (Updated for Full Platform)

| Tier | Safety Value | Ops Value (H2) | Coordination Value (H3) | Price Anchor |
|------|-------------|----------------|------------------------|-------------|
| Starter ($99) | Written programs, inspections, toolbox talks | Time tracking, basic daily logs | — | < one day of a safety consultant |
| Professional ($299) | + Mock OSHA, voice, photo, morning brief | + Full daily logs, quality inspections | — | Replaces $1,275/mo in separate tools |
| Business ($499) | + Prequal, EMR, GC portal | + Equipment, expanded sub mgmt | — | 1/12th of a safety director + ops manager |

## 7. Go-to-Market Pricing

### Horizon 1: Launch Phase (Months 1-6)

- **Lead with safety** — this is the wedge. "AI Safety Director for $99/month."
- **14-day Professional trial** (no credit card) to demonstrate AI features.
- **Promotional:** 30% annual discount for first 500 subscribers.
- **Target:** 10,000 free accounts, 350 paid customers, $80K MRR.

### Horizon 2: Expansion Phase (Months 6-12)

- **Announce operational features as "included at no extra cost"** — this is a marketing event, not a pricing change. "Kerf now includes daily logs, time tracking, and quality inspections — at the same price."
- **Drive the consolidation narrative:** "Cancel ClockShark. Cancel Raken. You don't need them anymore."
- **Track app consolidation rate** (target: 2+ tools replaced per customer).
- **Track features per customer** (target: 3.5+ modules actively used).
- **Referral program:** 1 month free for both parties.

### Horizon 3: Platform Phase (Months 12-18)

- **Evaluate price increase** once RFIs, scheduling, and change orders ship.
- **Enterprise sales motion** for 20+ project contractors.
- **GC-driven adoption** — free GC portal, monetize through subs.

### Horizon 4: Intelligence Phase (Year 2-3)

- **Premium intelligence tier** — predictive analytics, industry benchmarks, insurance carrier integrations could justify a premium add-on or higher Enterprise pricing.
- **Volume discounts** for multi-company groups.
- **Insurance-subsidized pricing** — carriers offset subscription costs for policyholders.

### Discount Strategy

| Type | Amount | Conditions |
|------|--------|------------|
| Annual billing | 25% | All tiers |
| Early adopter (launch) | 30% | First 500 annual subscribers |
| Referral | 1 month free | Both parties, any tier |
| Nonprofit / veteran-owned | 15% | Verification required |
| Trade association member | 10% | ABC, AGC, NECA, MCAA |

## 8. Risk Analysis

### Risk 1: Per-Project Model Limits Revenue Per Customer

**Risk:** A 50-person crew pays $1,200/mo on SafetyCulture (safety only) vs $299/mo on Kerf (safety + daily logs + time + quality).

**Mitigation:** The platform consolidation thesis means our ARPU ceiling is much higher than a safety-only tool. As customers use more modules, retention approaches 100%. LTV compensates for lower monthly price. And pricing headroom exists — at Horizon 3, $349-549/mo is still 1/10th of Procore.

### Risk 2: Giving Away Horizon 2 Features Leaves Money on the Table

**Risk:** Including daily logs, time tracking, and quality at no extra cost means we forgo $49-99/mo per add-on.

**Mitigation:** The "one tool, not six" positioning is the product's core identity. Charging per-module undermines it. The real revenue play is higher retention (multi-feature users churn 3-5x less in SaaS benchmarks), higher conversion (the value proposition is overwhelming at $299/mo for everything), and a justified price increase at Horizon 3.

### Risk 3: Competitors Bundle Faster

**Risk:** SafetyCulture or Safesite adds daily logs and time tracking, matching Kerf's breadth.

**Mitigation:** SafetyCulture is horizontal (100+ industries), losing $36M/year, and just had CEO turnover. They are unlikely to go deep on construction operations. Safesite is small and insurance-focused. Neither has the voice-first, foreman-first field experience. And first-mover advantage in data compounds daily.

### Risk 4: Business Tier Price Reduction

**Risk:** Reducing from $599 to $499 loses ~17% per Business customer.

**Mitigation:** With Horizon 2 features included, $499 for safety + daily logs + time + quality + GC portal + EMR is objectively better value than the current $599 for safety alone. Conversion from Professional should increase.

### Risk 5: AI Feature Commoditization

**Risk:** AI safety features become standard within 12-18 months.

**Mitigation:** The vision document identifies five compounding moat layers — knowledge graph, bilingual voice model, data network, workflow embedding, and ecosystem lock-in. AI generation is the wedge, not the moat. By the time competitors ship AI generation, Kerf needs to be on predictive analytics with data they cannot replicate.

### Risk 6: Procore Moves Downmarket

**Risk:** Procore launches a "Procore Lite" at $500/month targeting small contractors.

**Mitigation:** Procore's DNA is office-first, not field-first. Their minimum viable product still requires project management overhead that a 25-person crew doesn't need. Kerf is built for the foreman, not the project manager. Different user, different product, different price.
