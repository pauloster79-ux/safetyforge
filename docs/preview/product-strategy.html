1. Executive Summary

Kerf is the graph-intelligent construction business platform for contractors who need Procore but cannot afford it. We started with safety compliance and are expanding into daily logs, quality, time tracking, estimating, job costing, sub management, and scheduling. Every expansion is powered by a knowledge graph that connects every worker, certification, project, regulation, cost line, daily log, conversation, and uploaded document into one intelligent system.

Our wedge is safety. Our moat is the knowledge graph. Our interface is conversational. Our endgame is the operating system for construction.

The construction management software market is $10.6B globally (8.9% CAGR). 750,000+ small contractors in the US alone are massively underserved. Internationally — UK, Canada, Australia, Europe — the picture is the same or worse. We replace all of it, globally.

3.3 Global Competitive Landscape NEW

US market:

  • JobTread ($199/mo) — strongest financial management competitor. Excellent estimate-to-budget flow. 11,944% revenue growth. But: no safety, no voice, no AI intelligence, relational database limits agentic capability.
  • Procore ($30K+/yr) — does everything, prices out small contractors.
  • Point solutions — Raken, ClockShark, Fieldwire, SafetyCulture, CompanyCam. Each does one thing. None connect.

International:

  • UK: Fieldwire + PlanRadar for PM. HandsHQ + swiftRMS for RAMS. Safety and ops completely separate. No integrated platform.
  • Australia/NZ: SafetyCulture (1.5M users) — checklist engine, no graph, no financial connection. HammerTech slow and expensive.

  • Europe: PlanRadar strong on docs, no financial tools, no safety depth.
  • All markets: Nobody connects safety to financial outcomes. Nobody has a knowledge graph. Nobody has conversational estimating.

8. One-tool consolidation. The integrated intelligence is the product, not any single feature.

7. Key Risks and Mitigations

AI generates incorrect safety content [Critical] — Disclaimer + review checkbox. Confidence scoring. Professional liability insurance.
Expanding too fast dilutes safety quality [High] — Safety remains foundation. Every expansion reuses existing engines. Quality gates enforced.
Field adoption failure [High] — Voice input, one-thumb, 2-minute tasks. Monthly ride-alongs. Kill unused features.
Procore moves downmarket [High] — Per-seat enterprise architecture cannot serve 15-person crews at $99/month without cannibalising $30K+ customers.
Auto-derived financials are inaccurate NEW [High] — If auto-coded time entries or change order detection are wrong, trust dies. Require confirmation on consequential actions. Build accuracy from contractor's own data.
International regulatory encoding errors NEW [High] — Wrong compliance = real-world harm. Risk-stratified human verification (100% for life-safety rules). Start with English-speaking markets.
Point solutions bundle together [Medium] — API integration between independent tools is always worse than one codebase.
Contractors distrust AI [Medium] — "AI-assisted, expert-reviewed." Show citations. Offer consultant review add-on.
Time tracking perceived as surveillance [Medium] — Principle 7: no surveillance. Hours for payroll, not speed.
JobTread acquires safety company NEW [Medium] — Relational architecture limits graph-based intelligence. Bolting on safety is a feature; graph-native intelligence is an architecture.

9. The Moat

Layer 1: Knowledge graph architecture. Every entity connected to every related entity. Regulations, certifications, workers, projects, costs, conversations, documents — all traversable. Competitors on relational databases cannot replicate without re-architecting.
Layer 2: Safety-first architecture. Every expansion enriched by safety data. No competitor built from safety outward. Safety is the regulatory wedge; the graph connects it to everything.
Layer 3: Conversation memory. NEW Every chat, every voice interaction, every decision and its reasoning — accumulated in the graph. After 50 jobs, the agent knows the business better than any spreadsheet. Cannot be exported or replicated.
Layer 4: Cross-feature data network. Daily log → safety. Time → job costing. Estimates → tasks → cost tracking. Quality uses the same engine. Sub scoring uses safety data. Change orders assemble from daily log evidence. The integrated intelligence IS the product.
Layer 5: Multilingual conversational model. Construction audio in English, Spanish, Polish, French, Mandarin, and more. Compounds with every interaction across every market.
Layer 6: Workflow embedding. When the contractor's entire day runs through one conversational interface, switching costs are enormous. You cannot go back to forms after talking to an agent that knows your business.
Layer 7: GC/sub network + insurance ecosystem. Multi-sided network effects compound. Each participant increases every other's value and switching cost.

Every product decision should trace back to this strategy. When we debate what to build, we ask: does it make the graph more connected, the intelligence more useful, and the platform stickier? If it creates a new silo, it does not belong.
